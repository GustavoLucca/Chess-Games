The Morra Gambit

A GM upset at the hands of Morra Gambit expert IM Marc Esserman

In chess, highly exceptional players can obtain titles, determined by your rating and awarded by the international chess federation. The two top chess titles are IM, or International Master, and GM, or Grand Master. Despite the fact that these two titles are the most difficult to obtain, GM's are generally regarded as being much better players than IM's and are expected to beat them regularly. This is why it is such a surprise that IM Marc Esserman managed to beat his GM opponent, although this feat is regarded as even more impressive once we consider that he beat him by playing the Morra Gambit. Like the Evan's Gambit, the Morra Gambit is a chess opening where you sacrifice material, in this case a pawn, to gain an initiative. The opening is named after Pierre Morra, the first person to publish educational material on the Morra gambit in the 1950s. Also like the Evans Gambit, it has a shaky reputation, widely considered by most GM's to be unsound. Despite this, IM Marc Esserman has made it his main weapon of choice against the Sicilian with White, and is considered to be the main advocate in favor of the opening and its soundness in the present day.
