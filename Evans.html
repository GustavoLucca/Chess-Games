The Evans Gambit

A fiery surprise by Garry Kasparov against Viswanathan Anand

The Evans Gambit is a sacrificial opening first played by Captain Evans in the 1820s. The premise of the opening (and of gambits in general) is that White sacrifices a pawn in order to gain initiative, i.e. to develop his pieces before his opponent does. For the larger part of the 1900s, this opening was believed to be theoretically unsound, meaning that it has been proven to be inferior compared to other options that White has in the opening. This is why it was a great surprise to see Garry Kasparov, current world chess champion, play this suspicious opening against Viswanathan Anand, widely considered the second-best chess player in the world. This wasn't during a casual game for fun either; it was played in a professional chess tournament, the 1995 Tal Memorial played in Riga, Latvia.
