The Dragondorf Defense

Hikaru Nakamura shocks his opponent with the rare Dragondorf Defense

Previously, we've seen two opening types called "Gambits" where material is sacrificed for some sort of compensation, usually for initiative. However, to play agressive chess, it is not necessary to sacrifice material. The Dragondorf is a seldom-seen variation of the Sicilian Defense played by Black where Black combines the Dragon variation with the Najdorf variation of the Sicilian. It's a very obscure and rarely-seen variation of the Sicilian, so much so that I could not find any information on its history online. The general idea is to create a pawn storm on the Queenside after a6 and b5, and to occupy the semi-open c column with your Rook to create a menacing attack against White's castled king. Black's bishop on g7 and strategic Knight placement also helps for this purpose. Meanwhile, White should attempt an attack on the Kingside and try to arrive to the king before Black does. Due to this being a rare opening in top play, it was surprising to see Hikaru Nakamura, widely regarded as one of the best chess players in the world, play this in the French League against his GM opponent.
